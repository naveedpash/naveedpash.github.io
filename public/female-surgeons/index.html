Am I Biased?

What I find girl surgeons do differently

When making a diagnosis, they tend to think communally. Meaning they will consider what their seniors will think in reaction to each possible diagnosis.

  • Extra investigations to rule out obscure diagnoses
  • Escalation of care to avoid scrutiny

Basically I find that women in surgery adopt the stresses in any particular environment as a fact of life. They consider it impossible to question the system that is already in place. In many ways this is a good thing: training involves absorption of vasts amount of knowledge without question so that its application is in accordance with the notions of the seniors who teach.

This also means that the capacity to disagree with the senior is limited.

Women take things on themselves. This is good because life becomes a checklist of items that need to be fulfilled in order to achieve a goal. This limits systemic thinking. Delegation of tasks to a junior rather than to the system that was originally designed to support it.
